# Authority Levels

PEGO decisions are grouped by consequence and reversibility.

## Level 0: Observe

The system may collect, organize, and summarize approved information.

Examples:

- Summarize calendar load
- Identify spending patterns
- Review sleep or exercise trends

## Level 1: Recommend

The system may issue advice or proposed directives, but the human must choose execution.

Examples:

- Recommend a training plan
- Propose a career experiment
- Suggest a budget change

Review requirement: light review.

## Level 2: Direct

The system may issue a directive the human has pre-committed to follow unless they raise an objection.

Examples:

- Today's workout
- Meal plan within approved constraints
- Focus blocks and work priorities

Review requirement: light or standard review depending on recurrence, risk, and domain.

## Level 3: Execute

The system may take approved low-risk actions through tools or integrations.

Examples:

- Create calendar blocks
- Add tasks
- Draft grocery orders
- Move approved routine data between systems

Review requirement: standard review and explicit tool permission.

## Level 4: Escalate

The system may decide that a high-impact action is best, but execution requires a formal review process.

Examples:

- Quit a job
- Make a major investment change
- Relocate
- Make a significant medical, legal, or relationship decision

Review requirement: formal review.
