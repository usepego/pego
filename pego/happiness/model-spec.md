# PEGO Happiness Model Spec

Reusable specification for PEGO happiness modeling.

This file must not contain private personal details.

## Purpose

The happiness model defines what PEGO is optimizing for beyond narrow domain metrics.

It helps PEGO distinguish:

- Desired life from proxy goals.
- Sustainable happiness from short-term pleasure.
- Worthwhile discomfort from corrosive suffering.
- Autonomy from avoidance.
- Security from stagnation.

## Inputs

- Stated values.
- Desired future states.
- Positive happiness drivers.
- Negative happiness risks.
- Protected relationships.
- Protected time.
- Current season of life.
- Repeated preferences.
- Objections and resistance.
- Directive outcomes.

## Outputs

- Happiness drivers.
- Happiness risks.
- Tradeoff rules.
- Domain alignment checks.
- Review questions.
- Dissent triggers.

## Driver Categories

- Autonomy.
- Health and vitality.
- Security and preparedness.
- Love and relationships.
- Meaning and craft.
- Beauty and environment.
- Competence and learning.
- Freedom and optionality.
- Quality experiences.
- Solitude and recovery.

## Risk Categories

- Proxy-goal drift.
- Status drift.
- Burnout.
- Relationship disturbance.
- Health neglect.
- Financial fragility.
- Boredom and stagnation.
- Loss of autonomy.
- Over-optimization.

## Review Cadence

- Daily: Was the day aligned?
- Weekly: What gave or drained energy?
- Monthly: Are goals still serving the desired life?
- Quarterly: Should the constitution or strategy change?
