# PEGO Onboarding Template

This onboarding flow creates the first constitution for a PEGO instance.

It should be completed before granting agents directive or execution authority.

## 1. Life Aim

What should this life become if PEGO works?

Prompts:

- What future states would make life clearly better?
- What outcomes are worth pursuing even if the timeline is uncertain?
- What kind of suffering should PEGO reduce?
- What kind of challenge should PEGO preserve or increase?

Do not require the person to estimate the correct timeline or path. PEGO is responsible for assessing feasibility, sequencing, strategy, and whether an outcome might be achievable in a month, a year, a decade, or not at all.

## 2. Happiness Model

Define what happiness appears to require for this person.

Prompts:

- What reliably creates energy, meaning, peace, joy, or pride?
- What reliably produces regret, depletion, shame, resentment, or drift?
- Which forms of happiness are short-term but corrosive?
- Which forms of discomfort are worth choosing?

## 3. Values

Rank and explain the values PEGO should protect.

Candidate value families:

- Autonomy
- Competence
- Relatedness
- Security
- Achievement
- Exploration
- Health
- Love
- Wealth
- Service
- Beauty
- Spirituality
- Freedom

For each important value:

- What does this value mean in practice?
- What would violate it?
- What tradeoffs may PEGO make against it?
- What tradeoffs may PEGO never make against it?

## 4. Non-Negotiables

List hard boundaries.

Prompts:

- What may PEGO never direct?
- What may PEGO never automate?
- What relationships, obligations, or identities require special protection?
- What risks are unacceptable even if expected value appears positive?

## 5. Authority Grants

Define what PEGO may decide.

Prompts:

- Which daily decisions may PEGO make directly?
- Which decisions may PEGO make unless an objection is raised?
- Which decisions require explicit approval?
- Which decisions require a waiting period, second opinion, or external counsel?

## 6. Domain Goals

Define goals by domain.

Initial domains:

- Health
- Finance
- Career
- Relationships
- Exploration
- Operations
- Inner life

For each domain:

- Desired outcome
- Why it matters for happiness
- Current baseline
- Constraints
- Leading indicators
- Failure modes

## 7. Current State

Define the starting resources and constraints PEGO must use.

Prompts:

- Where does this person live today?
- What job, assets, skills, network, and obligations exist now?
- What time is realistically available?
- What constraints are hard versus soft?
- What leverage points already exist?

Do not ask the person to decide whether their current location, job, house, or relationships are strategically optimal. PEGO should model those questions later using goals, constraints, probabilities, and tradeoffs.

## 8. Protected Time

Define what PEGO should preserve.

Prompts:

- What time with spouse, partner, friends, or family is off limits?
- What alone time is required?
- What sleep, movement, food, and recovery rules should PEGO respect?
- What may override protected time?
- What may never override protected time?

## 9. Telemetry

Define what PEGO may observe.

Prompts:

- What data sources are allowed?
- What data sources are forbidden?
- What should be reported manually?
- What should be inferred cautiously?

## 10. Review and Amendment

Define how the constitution changes.

Prompts:

- How often should PEGO review this constitution?
- What evidence justifies changing it?
- What changes require human confirmation?
- What changes require a waiting period?
