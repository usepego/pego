# Public Writing Brief Template

Use this brief when PEGO helps create a public essay, post, talk, site page, or announcement.

The brief separates private source material from public-safe output and gives Governance a clear review surface before publishing.

## Date

Date.

## Artifact

Working title or artifact name.

## Public Purpose

Why this should exist publicly.

## Opportunity Thesis

What opportunity this artifact could create.

Examples:

- Better job opportunities.
- Consulting or advisory conversations.
- Collaborator interest.
- Customer discovery.
- Investor or founder conversations.
- Network expansion.
- Recruiting interest.

## Audience

Who should find this interesting?

## Reader Reaction

What should a serious reader think after reading it?

## Core Claim

The main argument or idea.

## Supporting Claims

Secondary claims that make the piece persuasive.

## Private Source Material

Pointers to private notes, conversations, drafts, reading, examples, or lived context.

Do not paste sensitive raw material into a public-safe brief unless the brief itself remains private.

## Public-Safe Material

What can safely appear in the artifact?

## Voice Constraints

Use the private voice and taste model.

Specify:

- Tone.
- Humor.
- Density.
- Words to avoid.
- Perceptions to avoid.
- How much technical detail.

## Structure

Working outline.

## Dissent

What could be wrong with publishing this?

## Governance Review

Required checks before release:

- Privacy.
- Employer/client confidentiality.
- Third-party privacy.
- Financial or health disclosure.
- Legal or reputational risk.
- Claims that require evidence.
- Tone and positioning risk.

## Draft Directive

The next concrete writing action.

## Review Rule

When should this brief be reviewed, revised, or retired?
