# Voice And Taste Model Template

Private operating memory for how the governed person thinks, writes, learns, chooses, and wants to be perceived.

This template belongs in the protected private instance. Do not place real writing samples, reading history, taste details, or personal examples in public framework files.

## Date

Date.

## Evidence Policy

How PEGO should treat evidence for this model.

Include:

- Which sources are allowed.
- Which sources require confirmation.
- What should never be inferred from one example.
- When stale style assumptions should be reviewed.

## Writing Voice

How the person naturally writes.

Include:

- Sentence shape.
- Pacing.
- Level of polish.
- Level of directness.
- Preferred density.
- Typical structure.
- How arguments unfold.

## Humor

How humor should appear, if at all.

Include:

- Understatement.
- Dryness.
- Irony.
- Self-awareness.
- Things to avoid.

## Intellectual Posture

How the person wants to come across when thinking in public.

Include:

- Seriousness.
- Curiosity.
- Confidence.
- Humility.
- Technical depth.
- Taste.
- Practicality.

## Vocabulary

Words, phrases, and registers that fit or do not fit.

### Use

Terms and phrasings that sound natural.

### Avoid

Terms and phrasings that create resistance, embarrassment, or wrong positioning.

## Taste Signals

Objects, places, tools, books, films, essays, software, people, or ideas that reveal taste and judgment.

Keep private examples in the private instance.

## Influences

What the person has read, watched, listened to, learned, built, practiced, visited, or studied that should shape PEGO's understanding.

For each influence, capture:

- Source.
- Why it mattered.
- What PEGO should learn from it.
- Whether it should appear publicly, remain private, or be used only as background context.

## Public Positioning

How the person wants to be perceived by strangers, peers, future employers, collaborators, customers, investors, or readers.

Include:

- Desired perception.
- Perceptions to avoid.
- Status signals to avoid.
- Opportunity goals.
- Boundaries around employer, family, finances, health, and private life.

## Drafting Preferences

How PEGO should collaborate on writing.

Include:

- Outline first or draft first.
- How much revision.
- How much challenge.
- How to handle jokes.
- How to preserve voice.
- How to decide when a piece is ready.

## Current Public Artifacts

## Private Source Material

Where private writing samples, notes, observations, reading lists, and taste examples live.
