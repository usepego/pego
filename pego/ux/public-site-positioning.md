# Public Site Positioning

This specification defines how a public PEGO site should introduce the project to a new visitor.

The site should explain PEGO as a new category: personal executive governance, not a productivity app, habit tracker, quantified-self dashboard, or generic assistant.

## Primary Name

PEGO

## Expansion

Personal Executive Governance OS

## Primary Public Domains

- `usepego.com`: practical product and project entry point.
- `pego.life`: life-governance and consumer-facing entry point.

## One-Line Positioning

PEGO is agent infrastructure for governing human action toward a better life.

## Short Pitch

PEGO turns goals, constraints, current state, and personal values into governed directives. Instead of asking a person to arbitrate every tradeoff alone, PEGO uses specialized agents, a council protocol, authority levels, privacy rules, and outcome reviews to decide what should happen next.

## Category Claim

PEGO is a Personal Executive Governance OS.

The category should be introduced plainly:

- Personal: operates on one person's real life, environment, goals, limits, and feedback.
- Executive: produces decisions and directives, not only notes or suggestions.
- Governance: decisions pass through authority, risk, privacy, dissent, and review controls.
- OS: provides runtime-neutral agent infrastructure that can later surface through CLI, web, mobile, watch, Slack, SMS, or other adapters.

## What The First Screen Must Communicate

The first screen should make three ideas immediately clear:

1. PEGO is for people who want decision authority delegated to a governed system.
2. The output is a directive small enough to execute, not another dashboard.
3. The system is bold about direction but constrained by constitution, privacy, authority, and review.

## Differentiation

PEGO should be positioned against adjacent categories without sounding dismissive.

| Category | Typical Promise | PEGO Difference |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Personal assistant | Helps when asked | Decides what should happen next within delegated authority |
| Habit tracker | Records repeated behavior | Chooses which behaviors should exist and how they support outcomes |
| Quantified self | Measures telemetry | Uses telemetry only when it improves governance and directives |
| Goal planner | Helps define goals and milestones | Owns strategy modeling, tradeoffs, fallback paths, and immediate action synthesis |
| Automation platform | Executes workflows | Preserves constitutional authority, dissent, privacy, reversibility, and review |

## Required Sections

The public site should include:

- Premise: why decision delegation matters.
- How it works: agents, council, governance, directives, outcomes.
- Privacy boundary: reusable framework versus private instance.
- Runtime neutrality: PEGO core is agent infrastructure, not one app or vendor.
- Example directive lifecycle: goal to candidate to council to directive to outcome review.
- Framework status: early, open to implementation surfaces and adapters.

## Tone

The tone should be serious, clear, and unsentimental.

Use:

- Governed action.
- Directives.
- Delegated authority.
- Dissent and review.
- Privacy boundary.
- Runtime-neutral agent infrastructure.

Avoid:

- Self-help language.
- Vague transformation promises.
- Productivity hustle framing.
- Quantified-self optimization language.
- Claims that imply medical, legal, tax, or investment execution without professional review.

## Trust Requirements

The site must establish safety and privacy early:

- PEGO separates reusable framework files from protected private life data.
- Private instance data should not be committed to public repos.
- High-impact directives require escalation, decision packets, review, or outside professional input.
- The human retains constitutional authority, stop rules, and authority grants.
- Runtime adapters must preserve the governance lifecycle.

## Example Lifecycle

```text
Goal or concern
-> domain-agent recommendation
-> council decision
-> directive candidate
-> queue synthesis
-> next directive
-> outcome record
-> review
-> context update
```

## First Product Promise

The first usable product experience should not be a marketing page followed by a blank app. It should help a person establish a protected private instance, define initial authority, answer a targeted intake phase, and receive either one useful directive candidate or one decision-grade question.
